Opening Night at the Circus

My four years in university were marked by a series of acts and gags with the characters that filled my days. There was one friend, who I remember quite clearly now, to be a spectator to the circus. Riko lived directly across from me during our first year of university. We were in a co-ed dormitory, and the floor was lined with jesters, trapezists, conjoined twins, and other freaks. The first day at university was hot, muggy, and the crushing humidity in the air just made me want to take a long nap in front of an oscillating fan. Head to toe, head to toe, a breeze would rock me to sleep. We found no such refuge. The circus was in town, and furniture clogged the hallways. Hovering parents lingered by half open doorways, giving parting advice which would soon be forgotten by sage children that already knew better. Riko and I participated in the performance, as it was the right thing to do.

I laid in my dorm bed without sheets. My moist back stuck to the plastic mattress, sweat pooling underneath me. I watched Riko unpack her things through our doors left wide open in a futile attempt to encourage airflow. She had a hunched posture and reminded me of how a stalk of scallion appears when left too long in a jar on the window sill. Watching her while I lay sideways with the whole world rotated 90 degrees, I imagined her to be an alien in an unnatural world. Riko’s eyes were sunken and large, and she would periodically pause to gaze at nothing. Nothing was just something I couldn’t see. She was deeply considering something orders of magnitude beyond my comprehension. She took a sharp inhale and held a bubble in her mouth shortly before returning to the mound of boxes in front of her. From the moment I noticed the focused look in Riko’s eyes, as if she was intermittently leaving our world to check on her own private reality, I felt a deep curiosity to see what secrets swirled in the seas of that parallel dimension. She did not yet know my name.
